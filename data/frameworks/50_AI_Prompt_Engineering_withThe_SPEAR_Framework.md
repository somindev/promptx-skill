# 

AI Prompt Engineering with

The SPEAR Framework

The SPEAR Framework, created by [AI consultant and international keynote speaker Britney Muller](https://britneymuller.com/), is built on 5 simple steps that make prompt engineering effective and accessible. The emphasis is on keeping it simple—don’t overthink! By following SPEAR, you can develop clear, focused prompts to achieve the best results from AI.

SPEAR, which stands for Start, Provide, Explain, Ask, and Rinse & Repeat, structures your requests so you can communicate effectively without unnecessary complexity. This approach guides you to clearly define your needs, ensuring the AI delivers precise and helpful responses.

Britney’s philosophy is rooted in clarity and efficiency, helping prompt engineers avoid over-complication. By following the SPEAR Framework’s simple steps, you can craft tailored prompts for clear, actionable AI responses without overthinking.

## 

SPEAR Framework Overview

  * **Start:** Define the problem or task you want the AI to help with.
    * **Provide:** Include specific examples or format guidance to shape the AI’s response.
      * **Explain:** Describe the situation as if explaining it to a person, giving necessary context.
        * **Ask:** State the precise question or main request for the AI to focus on.
          * **Rinse & Repeat:** Review and adjust the prompt as needed to achieve the desired outcome.

__




### Prompt example using the SPEAR Framework

Suppose you’re organizing a team-building event for remote employees. Here’s how a SPEAR-structured prompt might look:

Start: Help me design a virtual team-building event for a remote team. Provide: Include activities that require minimal setup and can be done via video call. Explain: The team consists of 12 members from different departments who rarely interact. The goal is to strengthen team bonds and foster a collaborative spirit. Ask: Suggest a 1-hour event agenda with interactive activities. Rinse & Repeat: Review the suggestions, refine details, and clarify further if needed.

## 

Strengths and Weaknesses of the SPEAR Framework

### Strengths

  * **Simplicity:** An easy-to-remember sequence that emphasizes clarity.
  * **Focus on Essentials:** Directly addresses key information AI needs to know.
  * **Efficiency:** Reduces unnecessary steps, making it quick to apply across different tasks.



### Weaknesses

  * **Limited for Complex Scenarios:** May lack depth for highly complex prompts or multi-step tasks.
  * **Reliance on Iteration:** Some tasks may require more rounds of refinement to reach an ideal outcome.



## 

When should you use the SPEAR framework?

Ideal applications of the framework

The SPEAR framework is especially effective for tasks where clarity and simplicity are crucial. It’s ideal for scenarios like content strategy development, market research, customer support prompts, and basic analytical requests.
