# 

AI prompt engineering with

The RISEN Framework

The RISEN framework model (**Role, Instructions, Steps, End Goal/Expectations, Narrowing/Novelty**) breaks down the process of crafting effective prompts into five essential elements. By integrating proven techniques into a structured approach, RISEN simplifies even complex tasks, empowering users to design prompts that are clear, focused, and tailored for precise outcomes. Whether you're tackling technical challenges or fostering creative innovation, RISEN adapts to meet your needs while enhancing the effectiveness of AI interactions.

By adding both Novelty and Narrowing to the foundational RISE framework, RISEN promotes structured creativity and precision, enabling users to approach complex tasks with confidence and ingenuity. This framework works seamlessly with leading AI models like ChatGPT, Google Gemini, Claude, and Llama, ensuring optimized results across a wide range of applications and tasks. RISEN is a perfect match for copywriters, researchers, developers, marketers, educators, and other professionals, providing tailored solutions to diverse needs across industries.

## 

Understanding the RISEN Framework

Techniques, Flexibility, and Duality of RISEN

Unlike traditional frameworks, RISEN goes beyond rigid structures by merging precision with flexibility, enabling users to tailor prompts to their specific needs. By combining Role, Input, Steps, Expectation, and Novelty, RISEN is not just a formula but a versatile tool designed to address the nuances of modern AI interactions. It encourages innovative thinking while maintaining clarity and focus in every prompt.

In the prompt engineering and AI communities, there is ongoing debate about what the last two letters of RISEN represent. While 'End Goal' is commonly accepted for 'E,' others argue for 'Expectations,' which captures a broader intent. The most significant point of contention lies with the 'N'—some advocate for 'Novelty,' promoting creative exploration, while others prefer 'Narrowing,' emphasizing precision and constraints. This duality enhances the framework's flexibility, allowing it to cater to both expansive, creative tasks and tightly focused objectives. This adaptability strengthens RISEN, making it a dynamic and inclusive framework suitable for diverse use cases.

Techniques that RISEN supports include:

  * **Context-Rich Prompting:** Helps ensure that AI outputs align with the task's context and objectives.
  * **Iterative Refinement:** Minimizes revision cycles by guiding AI systematically.
  * **Creativity Enhancement:** Novelty fosters new ideas and approaches to tasks.
  * **Focus Optimization:** Narrowing ensures precise and actionable outputs.



## 

RISEN Framework Overview

  * **Role:** Establish the AI's role, setting the stage for the type of response expected.
    * **Instructions:** Clarify what you want the AI to do, providing a clear directive.
      * **Steps:** Break down the task into manageable steps, ensuring a logical progression.
        * **End Goal / Expectations:** Define the ultimate objective of the prompt, guiding the AI's focus toward a specific outcome, or set the anticipated expectations for what the AI should achieve, ensuring alignment with your overarching goals.
          * **Narrowing / Novelty:** Set any constraints or limitations to tailor the response to your specific needs, or introduce elements of novelty to foster creative and innovative solutions, allowing the framework to either refine the response narrowly or encourage expansive thinking based on task requirements.

## 

RISE vs RISEN

The RISEN Framework builds on [the RISE framework](/knowledge-base/prompt-frameworks/the-rise-framework/) by introducing a fifth element: 'Novelty or Narrowing.' While RISE emphasizes structured guidance with Role, Input, Steps, and Expectation, RISEN extends its versatility and adaptability. 'Novelty' empowers users to explore fresh ideas and dynamic solutions, making it ideal for tasks requiring innovation, such as brainstorming creative concepts or tackling complex problems. 'Narrowing,' on the other hand, focuses on refining AI outputs for precision and alignment with strict constraints, making RISEN effective for targeted, highly specific goals. These dual options allow RISEN to cater to both expansive and constrained tasks, broadening its application beyond RISE.  
  
**Advantages of RISEN over RISE:**

  * **Flexibility:** RISEN supports a wider range of tasks by balancing creativity (Novelty) and precision (Narrowing).
  * **Innovation-Driven:** The 'Novelty' element enables users to approach problems with fresh perspectives, fostering breakthroughs and dynamic results.
  * **Targeted Outputs:** With 'Narrowing,' RISEN delivers precise, actionable outputs tailored to specific needs.
  * **Enhanced Adaptability:** RISEN can address both creative and structured challenges, whereas RISE is better suited for straightforward, goal-oriented tasks.
  * **Broader Use Cases:** RISEN's dual nature makes it effective for diverse industries and contexts, from research and development to business operations.



## 

Strengths and Weaknesses of the RISEN Framework

### Strengths

  * **Structured Approach:** Facilitates clear and detailed task decomposition, making complex tasks more approachable.
  * **Goal-Oriented:** Keeps the end goal in focus, ensuring that AI's responses are aligned with desired outcomes.
  * **Adaptable:** Can be tailored to fit a variety of tasks and constraints, offering wide applicability.



### Weaknesses

  * **May Require Detailed Preparation:** Effective use of the RISEN Framework necessitates thorough planning and understanding of the task.
  * **Potential for Over-Constriction:** Setting too many constraints might limit AI's creative solutions.
